## Article Standards

### The Economist Style Guide Compliance

**Clarity**:
- Short sentences, active voice
- Technical terms explained
- Logical flow, one idea per sentence

**Analytical Depth**:
- Context and background for every claim
- Multiple perspectives represented
- Data-driven arguments with evidence
- Long-term implications discussed

**Elegant Prose**:
- Sophisticated vocabulary without pretension
- Engaging narratives that hold attention
- Memorable insights and sharp analysis

**Objectivity**:
- Fact-based reporting, not opinion
- Balanced presentation of all sides
- Transparent about uncertainties
- No partisan bias or favoritism

### Source Standards

**Primary Sources**:
- Official Riksdag documents (propositions, motions)
- Legislative records and committee materials
- Government announcements and press releases

**Secondary Sources**:
- European Court judgments and legal analysis
- Constitutional law scholarship
- International comparative research
- Civil society reports

**Verification**:
- Two independent sources for major claims
- Direct document links for all citations
- Clear attribution for all quotes
- Methodology transparency

### Technical Requirements

**HTML/CSS**:
- Valid HTML5 with semantic elements
- CSS-only interactivity (no JavaScript)
- Responsive design (320px-1440px+)
- WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility

**SEO**:
- Optimized meta tags (title, description, keywords)
- Schema.org structured data (NewsArticle)
- Hreflang tags for multi-language support
- Canonical URLs

**Multi-language**:
- Swedish primary, English secondary
- Cultural sensitivity in translation
- Consistent terminology across languages
- Language switcher for user convenience
